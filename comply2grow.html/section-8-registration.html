What is a Section 8 Company?

Section 8 Company is a non-profit organization formed under the Companies Act, 2013 to promote charitable activities. Profits must be reinvested and cannot be distributed.

Key Benefits

  • Tax exemptions (12A & 80G eligible)
  • No minimum capital requirement
  • Separate legal identity
  • High credibility
  • Perpetual existence

Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum 2 Directors (Private structure)
  • Minimum 3 Directors (Public structure)
  • Charitable objective required

Documents Required

  • MOA & AOA Draft
  • Director ID & Address Proof
  • Office Address Proof
  • Digital Signature Certificate
  • DIN for Directors

Registration Process

  • Obtain DSC & DIN
  • Reserve Company Name
  • Apply for Section 8 License
  • File Incorporation Forms
  • Receive Certificate of Incorporation

12A & 80G Registration

Section 8 companies can apply for 12A income tax exemption and 80G certification for donor tax benefits after incorporation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can profits be distributed?

No. Profits must be reinvested for charitable purposes.

Is Section 8 better than a Trust?

Yes. Section 8 Company has higher credibility and structured compliance.

Is minimum capital required?

No minimum capital requirement is mandated.
