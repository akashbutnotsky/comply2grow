What is OPC?

One Person Company (OPC) allows a single entrepreneur to operate a company with limited liability protection under the Companies Act, 2013.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Natural Person & Indian Citizen
  • Minimum 1 Director & 1 Nominee
  • Minimum Authorized Capital: ₹1,00,000
  • No financial activities like NBFC
  • Only one OPC per person

Documents Required

  • MOA & AOA
  • Nominee Consent (INC-3)
  • Director Consent (DIR-2)
  • Office Address Proof
  • Digital Signature Certificate

Registration Process

  • Obtain DSC
  • Apply for DIN
  • Name Reservation (SPICe+ Part A)
  • Draft MOA & AOA
  • File SPICe+ Forms
  • Receive Certificate of Incorporation

Advantages of OPC

  • Limited liability
  • Separate legal identity
  • Full control by owner
  • Reduced compliance burden
  • Perpetual succession

Post-Incorporation Compliance

OPC must file annual returns with ROC and maintain statutory compliance similar to private limited companies.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can OPC have more than one shareholder?

No. OPC can have only one shareholder.

Is OPC better than sole proprietorship?

Yes. OPC provides limited liability and separate legal identity.

Can OPC convert into Pvt Ltd?

Yes. OPC can be converted into Private Limited Company later.
