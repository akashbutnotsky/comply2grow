IAF vs Non-IAF Accreditation

Not all ISO certificates are equal. Your choice between IAF and Non-IAF determines your global eligibility and tender acceptance.

✔ Global Acceptance ✔ Govt Tender Approval ✔ Export Readiness ✔ Fraud Protection

What is IAF Accreditation?

The International Accreditation Forum (IAF) is the world body for Conformity Assessment. An IAF-accredited certificate means your ISO has been verified by a national board (like NABCB in India or UKAS in UK), making it valid in 100+ countries.

IAF vs Non-IAF Certification

IAF Accredited

  • ✔ Accepted in 100+ countries
  • ✔ Mandatory for Govt tenders
  • ✔ Strict international audits
  • ✔ Includes IAF MLA mark
  • ✔ Best for exports & MNC clients

Non-IAF

  • ✖ Limited to local use
  • ✖ Not valid for tenders
  • ✖ Minimal audit checks
  • ✖ No global accreditation
  • ✖ Suitable for internal use only

Which One Should You Choose?

  • Choose IAF if: You want to export, bid for Government tenders, or work with MNCs.
  • Choose Non-IAF if: You only need a certificate for internal process improvement or small local retail requirements.
  • Specialty Standards: Standards like FDA, CE, and SA 8000 are critical for US/EU market entry.
