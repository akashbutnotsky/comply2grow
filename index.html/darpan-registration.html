NGO Darpan Registration

NGO Darpan Registration is a vital compliance requirement for Non-Governmental Organisations (NGOs) and Voluntary Organisations (VOs) in India. Managed by the Government of India through the NITI Aayog NGO Darpan Portal, this registration provides official recognition, improves credibility, and enables access to government grants and schemes.

What is NGO Darpan Registration?

NGO Darpan Registration is the process of enrolling an NGO on the NGO Darpan portal of NITI Aayog. Launched in 2015, the portal acts as a centralised national database of NGOs, trusts, societies, and Section 8 companies operating in India.

Objectives of NGO Darpan Registration

  • Access to Government Grants & Schemes
  • Transparency & Accountability
  • Centralised NGO Database

Eligibility Criteria

  • Registered Trust
  • Registered Society
  • Section 8 Company

Documents Required

  • NGO Registration Certificate
  • NGO PAN Card
  • Address Proof
  • Details of Office Bearers

Benefits of NGO Darpan Registration

  • Direct engagement with government ministries
  • Mandatory for government grants
  • Enhanced credibility
  • Access to funding opportunities

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Darpan ID?
Darpan ID is a unique identification number issued to NGOs after registration.

Is Darpan Registration mandatory?
Yes, it is mandatory for NGOs applying for government grants and schemes.

How long does registration take?
Usually completed within a few working days subject to document accuracy.
