What is a Class 3 Digital Signature Certificate?

A Class 3 Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) is the highest level of digital authentication certificate in India. It verifies identity and enables secure, legally valid electronic transactions under the Information Technology Act, 2000.

Why is a DSC Required?

  • Income Tax Return (ITR) filing
  • ROC filings (DIR-3, AOC-4, MGT-7)
  • GST registration & returns
  • E-Tendering & E-Procurement
  • ICEGATE & Import Export filings
  • Patent & Trademark filing

Who Needs a Class 3 DSC?

  • Company Directors
  • Chartered Accountants & Professionals
  • Authorized Signatories
  • GST Registered Businesses
  • IEC & DGFT Applicants

Types of Digital Signature Certificates

  • Sign DSC – For signing documents
  • Encrypt DSC – For encrypting data
  • Sign & Encrypt DSC – For both purposes

Recent Updates (2025–2026)

  • Aadhaar-based eKYC Verification
  • Video Verification Option
  • 1, 2, or 3 Year Validity Options
  • USB Token / Secure Download / Cloud DSC

Documents Required

  • PAN Card
  • Aadhaar Card
  • Address Proof
  • Passport-size Photograph

Validity & Renewal

DSCs are valid for 1 to 3 years. Renewal requires fresh identity verification and must be completed before expiry to avoid disruption in statutory filings.

Benefits of Using a Class 3 DSC

  • Legally recognized across India
  • High-level encryption & security
  • Fully paperless process
  • Faster approvals & filings
  • Compatible with MCA, GST, Income Tax, EPFO, GEM, DGFT
